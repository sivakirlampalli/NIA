Welcome to the Anti-Cyber Terrorism Division's Forensic Investigation Tool. This tool helps in parsing social media feeds, capturing screenshots, and documenting evidence with precision.

About the Tool

The National Investigation Agency (NIA) has developed a specialized tool for forensic investigation officers. This tool automatically parses data from social media accounts under investigation and generates documented screenshots of posts, messages, timelines, friend lists, followers, account information, and more.

Key Features

  • Automatic parsing of social media data
  • Generation of documented screenshots
  • Support for multiple platforms
  • Android and Windows versions available

What is NIA?

The National Investigation Agency (NIA) is India's primary counter-terrorism law enforcement agency, established under the National Investigation Agency Act of 2008. It was formed in response to the 2008 Mumbai terror attacks, with the goal of addressing the growing threat of terrorism in India. The NIA is empowered to investigate and prosecute offenses related to terrorism, national security, and other serious crimes, such as human trafficking, organized crime, and offenses against nuclear facilities. The agency operates under the Ministry of Home Affairs and has the authority to take over cases from state police forces if they involve issues of national importance or cross-border implications.

The NIA functions as a centralized investigative body with jurisdiction across the entire country, enabling it to coordinate and conduct investigations without the limitations of state boundaries. The agency works closely with other national and international security and intelligence agencies to tackle terrorism and other serious crimes. Over the years, the NIA has been instrumental in investigating and solving high-profile cases, playing a crucial role in maintaining national security and ensuring justice is served in cases of terrorism and related offenses.
